This has a reference to the subject cited above.
In this connection, it is stated that your institution is approved by the PCI for the conduct
of Pharm.D Course. For considering final approval u/s 12 of the Pharmacy Act, 1948 for
the purpose of registration as a pharmacist, you are required to ensure the strict
compliance of the following prescribed requirements under Appendix-B of PDR-2008.
1. Teaching Faculty
Institutions conducting Pharm.D. Programme shall have all the teaching staff
required-
a) for B.Pharm course as specified in the SIF for B.Pharm course (SIF-B)
b) for Pharm.D course as per
‘Appendix-B’ with full compliance of following
statutory provisions of PDR-2008-
 Number (Department wise) as prescribed under regulation 3 iii)
 Qualification and experience as prescribed under regulation 3 iv)
 Subject wise specializations as prescribed under regulation 3 ii)
 All faculty shall be full time as per prescribed under regulation 3 i)

2. Training of Pharmacy Practice Faculty as per regulations 3 vi) of the PDR-2008.
a) Institution is required to ensure that the Pharmacy Practice Faculty including the
HOD is trained as per regulation 3 vi) of PDR-2008.
b) PCI recommends that Pharmacy Practice Faculty including HOD shall undergo at
least 1 Continuing Education Programme / Training Programme of minimum 3 days
duration every year and participation in atleast one seminar/conference every year.
Documentary evidence to be submitted alongwith SDF for consideration.

a) Department of Pharmacy Practice shall have the teaching faculty as per regulation

b) The Pharmacy Practice Department in the hospital shall be fully functional which
shall be evidenced from the Drug Information data, ward round, staff/student log
book etc.
c) HOD of Pharmacy Practice Department shall interact with the medical officers and
administrative head of the hospital and wherever possible with the patient to ensure
that the Pharmacy Practice Department in the hospital is effectively providing the
pharmaceutical services and the care.
4. Hospital Training
a) In this regard the attention is drawn to the Regulations 7, 12(4),16, 19 to 24 and
‘Appendix-C’ of the PDR-2008 prescribing the statutory position with regard to
clerkship and internship.
b) Please ensure that the best practices are followed with regard to hospital like -
 the space of 30 sq.ft./student and maximum of 1000 sq.ft. is available.
 the Pharmacy Practice Department is functional in the hospital.
 the Pharmacy Practice Lab. at the college is functional.
 the Pharamcy Practice Staff with qualification & experience are appointed at the
Hospital.
 the perceptors at the hospital are teaching the students.
 necessary records of case studies, group discussions etc. is available.

 books and other drug information materials are available at Hospital site.
 Drug Information Services are provided at the hospital. Records regarding the
number of queries received be maintained.
 additional books and journals are subscribed with regard to pharmacy practice.
 the intern shall maintain a record of work which shall be certified by the
preceptor (teacher practitioner) under whom the students is working.
 in the nutshell the following objectives of ward rounds / hospital internship as
prescribed under PDR-2008 shall be fulfilled-
i) Refining Clinical Skills -
 Exposure to different type of patient population.
 Various dosage forms and its administration.
 Detect ADR and Interactions.
 Medication errors monitoring.
 Learning about safe decision making - risk management.
 Poly pharmacy management.
 Drug and therapeutic information etc.
ii) Record Management -
 How health care records are documented.
 Discharge planning of the patient etc.
iii) Management skills -
 How to work effectively in a team.
 Time management.
 Communications skills etc.
iv) Responsibility towards patient -
 How to maintain dignity of patient.
 Patient counseling etc.
As the students of your college are already in the 6th year of Pharm.D and undergoing
internship, please note that you are required to apply in SIF D for consideration of final
approval of the Pharm.D course u/s 12 of the Pharmacy Act, 1948 for the purpose of
registration as a pharmacist and all statutory conditions prescribed under PDR-2008 shall
be strictly adhered to.
